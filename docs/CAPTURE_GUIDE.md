# Capture Guide

Good splats begin with good captures.

## Golden Rule

The computer can only reconstruct what it has seen.

If you never capture the back of an object, the back cannot be reconstructed reliably.

## Best First Capture

Use:

- 40–80 photos
- soft lighting
- full orbit
- stable background
- matte object
- visible texture

## Camera Movement

Move slowly.

Keep the object centered.

Maintain a fairly constant distance.

## Lighting

Use soft, even light.

Avoid:

- harsh sunlight
- flash
- deep shadows
- flickering lights

## Difficult Objects

Avoid for first tests:

- mirrors
- glass
- chrome
- glossy black objects
- transparent plastic
- water

## Small Objects

Place the object on a table.

Capture:

- one eye-level orbit
- one slightly higher orbit
- a few closeups if needed

## Rooms and Large Objects

Move gradually through the space.

Avoid spinning in place quickly.

Keep overlap between photos.

## People

People are difficult because they move.

Use bright light, fast capture, and ask the person to remain still.
